###  Stay Safe Online: Student Defense Checklist

Based on the research analysis of cybercrime trends in India, here is your actionable prevention checklist:

  * **Refrain from Over-Sharing:** Minimize the risk of hacking by not sharing "personal information on social networking sites".
  * **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Always employ 2FA for all online services to add an "extra layer of security" against stolen passwords.
  * **Verify the Source:** Avoid clicking links, downloading files, or opening email attachments from "unknown senders".
  * **Maintain Digital Hygiene:** Use "updated antivirus software" to protect your devices and networks from virus attacks.
  * **Audit the URL:** Double-check the "spelling of the website, the URL, and the HTTP address" before entering any sensitive data.
  * **Avoid Public Wi-Fi:** Refrain from using public Wi-Fi in places like "railway stations, airports, and hotels". These networks are highly susceptible to "man-in-the-middle attacks" where hackers can intercept your personal information.
  * **Be Selective with Connections:** Avoid befriending "unfamiliar individuals" on social media just to increase your follower count, as this jeopardizes your privacy.
  * **Report Cyber Frauds:** Do not hesitate to report incidents due to social stigma;"hesitance to report" only provides a boost to cybercriminal activities.
  * **Verify UPI Transactions:** Always remember that a UPI PIN is only required for sending money, not receiving it. If someone asks you to enter your PIN for a "refund" or "prize," it is a fraudulent attempt to "dupe you into supplying financial information".
  * **Audit Digital Sources:** Before performing any financial transaction, "double-check the spelling of the website, the URL, and the HTTP address" to ensure it is not a "phishing" site designed for "financial fraud".
