### **Task 5: Cybercrime Case Study**

**What the crime is:** Cybercrime is an "umbrella term" for various digital offenses and behaviors committed online.This specifically includes **Phishing** and **Identity Theft**. Phishing is a type of fraud where victims are "duped into supplying financial or personal information," such as usernames and passwords. Identity theft occurs when a criminal steals this personal data to "open new accounts" or engage in other illegal activities in the victim's name.

**How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):** 1. **The Approach:** Phishers use "social engineering and email spoofing" technologies to deceive their targets, often through spam messages or fraudulent calls.  
2. **The Hook:** Victims are enticed to "click on links, download files, or open email attachments" from unknown senders.  
3. **The Harvest:** By interacting with these sources, the victim unknowingly provides sensitive data to the attacker.  
4. **The Exploitation:** The stolen data is used to gain "unauthorised access" to computer systems or accounts, leading to hacking, financial fraud, or "personification".

**Who is usually targeted:** Indian youth (aged 15–29) are primary targets due to their habit of "sharing excessive personal information" on social media. While they are often "media-savvy," they frequently lack the "digital literacy skills" required to evaluate the real purpose of a message or navigate the digital landscape safely.

**Consequences:** The consequences include "economic disruption" and "psychological disorders". In 2021 alone, over **27 million Indian adults** faced cyber theft. Beyond financial loss, victims often experience "severe mental agony," emotional distress, and depression to the point of "self-harm".
