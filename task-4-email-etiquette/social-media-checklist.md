# Student Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist

| **DO'S (The Professional Way)** | **DON'TS (Common Pitfalls)** |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Do** keep your LinkedIn and GitHub updated with your latest academic projects. | **Don't** post confidential project code, internal university data, or exam papers. |
| **Do** use strict privacy settings to separate your personal life from your professional image. | **Don't** engage in "flame wars," cyberbullying, or aggressive arguments in public comment sections. |
| **Do** give proper credit to original creators when sharing code, digital art, or research. | **Don't** post content that could be interpreted as academic dishonesty or plagiarism. |
| **Do** enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all your social and academic platforms. | **Don't** click on "too good to be true" links, viral "quizzes," or third-party apps that scrape your data. |
| **Do** ask yourself: "Would I be okay with a future recruiter or my Professor seeing this?" | **Don't** use unprofessional handles, offensive bios, or explicit profile pictures on networking sites. |
